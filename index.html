My Journey Through Web Development.

What interests you in a career as a software developer?

I come from a creative background. As a kid I used to draw a lot. I studied Industrial design for 1 year before falling in love with music. I have played music professionally for many years. I am also a yoga instructor. I like technology and like the idea of being able to make web applications. I am very interested in usability and accessibility. Learning to code has been a completely new experience for me and I value that. I like learning new skills, but I can see a future and a career in web development. Even though it can be very technical, it also allows for some degree of creativity in how you can do things. I am dedicated and I pay attention to detail. I can also see that there is a huge potential for development and progression to different fields.

Why do you feel like Founders and Coders is the right place for you to train as a software developer?

I had been learning HTML, CSS and Javascript for a few months when a friend directed me to Founders and Coders. I believe he went through the same process about 7 years ago; he recommended I look at your website and follow up with the apprenticeship. I looked at the program and I found it was exactly what I was looking for. other courses I was looking into were expensive and I was saving up for one of those but then with you I didn't have to wait, I could just do the projects, get in the bootcamp and go into a paid apprenticeship.

Tell us something you struggled with during the application. How did you find help?

I have struggled with a lot of things, e.g. writing code, setting up github, using the terminal, you name it. My housemate is a web developer and he helps me and encourages me at times. The web is incredibly useful in finding information on how to perform a certain task. The meetups organised by Founders and Coders have been incredibly helpful. I have made new friends and have received help from them, but mostly being able to see that there is a community of people who are on the same path and to some extent the same level as you is relieving.

How will you contribute to FAC being a safe and inclusive community?

There should be no space for any form of discrimination in our community. We should contribute with our actions to help people feel safe, be able to express themselves in a respectful manner, and feel valued and appreciated.

What role do you usually take when working in a team?

I have experience being a teacher, so provided I have the knowledge I am particularly good at being coach, helping team members overcome obstacles. I think I am also a good observer and can offer a neutral perspective on issues that may arise within a team, and can offer help mending relationships associated with conflict.
